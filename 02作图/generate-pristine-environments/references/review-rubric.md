# Environment review rubric

Review the rendered image, not prompt compliance by assumption.

## Full-frame gate

1. **Intent:** correct place, era, function, geography, weather, view, and aspect ratio.
2. **Composition:** dominant mass, focal destination, readable circulation, balanced negative space.
3. **Depth:** distinct planes, convincing overlap and scale falloff, controlled atmospheric perspective.
4. **Camera:** coherent horizon, vanishing behavior, verticals, lens character, and crop.
5. **Light:** unified source direction, shadow softness, exposure, bounce, and contact.
   - For low-key work, verify shadow separation rather than demanding brightness: deepest black is limited, dark masses retain hue and gradients, and local contrast reveals the focal structure without lifting the whole frame.

## Local-detail gate

6. **Construction:** support, access, joints, repeated modules, thickness, gravity, and intersections.
7. **Materials:** distinct response, correct texture scale, localized wear, believable contact and moisture.
8. **Nature:** geology, vegetation grouping, water, clouds, wind, erosion, and accumulation.
9. **Finish:** clean gradients, intact silhouettes, controlled sharpening, no tiling, halos, duplicates, text, or AI residue.

## Decision

- Pass when the scene is usable and remaining issues are optional polish.
- Hard fail only when a defect breaks use, geometry, required content, light, materials, or focal readability.
- If hard failed, correct only the top one or two causal problems once.
- Reject a second pass that merely changes color, exposure, or decoration without solving those problems.

Use scale-appropriate judgment: distant background simplification is normal; melted focal architecture is not. Atmospheric softness is normal; uniform milk-white haze that erases depth is not.
